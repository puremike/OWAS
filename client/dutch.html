What is a Dutch Auction?

A Dutch auction is a fast-paced, descending-price auction where the auctioneer starts with a high asking price and gradually lowers it until a bidder accepts the current price. This creates urgency and encourages quick decision-making.

How It Works:

  • The auction starts at a high price.
  • The price drops in set intervals or continuously.
  • The first person to accept the current price wins the item.

Example Scenario:

Imagine a seller auctioning off 100 fresh flowers. The price starts at $5 per flower and drops by $0.25 every minute. Buyers wait for the price to drop but risk missing out as others can buy at any time. The auction ends as soon as all flowers are sold or the lowest price is reached.
